About

Stories That Take Flight

The Women's Free Flight Film Festival celebrates and empowers women pilots by sharing their stories, adventures, challenges, and achievements in free flight. We're building a lasting library of films that clubs, instructors, and organizations can use to welcome new pilots into the sport — proving that a powerful story from a newly rated pilot can shine as brightly as any big-budget expedition film.

Our mission: more visibility, more community, more women in the air.

About Us

Who's Behind This

Built by pilots, for pilots — with the community that's lifting more women into the air.

Julia Lieberman — Founder

Founder of Women of Speedflying and a passionate pilot. After learning to paraglide in 2021, Julia has hosted international women's events, run scholarships, written articles for USHPA, and most recently joined the USHPA Women's Committee — helping build a sponsored fund in alliance with the Free Flight Foundation.

In Partnership With the USHPA Women's Committee

This festival is proud to align with the USHPA Women's Committee, chaired by Violeta Jimenez — president of the Women's Committee and an internationally recognized cross-country (XC) champion. Together we're working to grow visibility, mentorship, and opportunity for women in free flight.

Enter

Submit Your Film

Submit any short film 2–20 minutes featuring a woman participating in and interacting with free flight — paragliding, hang gliding, speedflying, speedriding, miniwing, or hike-and-fly.

What we're looking for

Adventure, mentorship, personal journeys, comebacks, community, history, instructional experiences — all welcome. No extreme flying or professional production required. We prioritize storytelling over budget.

Requirements

  • 2–20 minutes (5–10 recommended)
  • U.S.-based pilot as the primary subject
  • MP4, 1080p+, H.264
  • English or English subtitles
  • Suitable for all audiences
  • You hold rights to any music used

The Fine Print

Rules & Terms

Please read these in full before submitting. By submitting, the entrant agrees to these terms.

Eligibility

  • Film features a woman as the primary subject
  • Focused on free flight activities
  • Pilot(s) based in the United States
  • Suitable for public audiences
  • Runtime 2–20 minutes

Technical & Screening

  • MP4, 1080p minimum, H.264
  • English or English subtitles (auto-captions not sufficient)
  • Screener via Vimeo (password), FilmFreeway upload, or unlisted YouTube (ads off)
  • Entrant holds rights to all music & content used

Rights & Ownership

Filmmakers retain full ownership of their films. By submitting, entrants grant the festival non-exclusive rights to screen the film at approved events and to use clips or trailers for promotion. Selected films may be included in a lasting library for outreach by clubs, instructors, and organizations. All decisions are made by the festival and its jury at their sole discretion.
